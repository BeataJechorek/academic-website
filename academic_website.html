About Me

I am an Associate Professor at the Institute of Economics at the Poznań University of Economics and Business in Poland. My research focuses on the institutional problems of labor markets, digital transformation, the future of work, remote working, and new industrial relations.

I have contributed to various highly ranked journals and my monographs have appeared with Routledge (Taylor & Francis) and Palgrave Macmillan. My book titled 'Institutional Determinants of the Polish Labor Market: A Theoretical and Empirical Study' received recognition as the top labor market publication in Poland by The Polish Academy of Science in 2016. Additionally, I was honored with the BISE Bank Award for my outstanding Ph.D. thesis.

In 2021, I was awarded a scholarship by the Polish National Agency for Academic Exchange (Bekker Programme), allowing me to spend 15 months at Northwestern University in the United States (ranked as the ninth-best university in the country by U.S. News & World Report). My research portfolio includes numerous projects funded by Polish institutions, including the National Science Centre, the Ministry of Science and Higher Education, and the National Centre for Research and Development, as well as European institutions such as the European Commission's DG REGIO and Eurofound.

I serve as a Council Member of the European Association for Evolutionary Political Economy (EAEPE) for the 2024–2025 and 2026–2027 terms.
